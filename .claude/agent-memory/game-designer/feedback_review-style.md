When asked to review a design, the user wants genuine adversarial critique, not validation. They explicitly frame review requests as "find problems" and "be direct and critical."

**Why:** The user's own words: "Your job is NOT to validate this design — your job is to find problems. Challenge the design choices." This is a deliberate stance, not a tone preference.

**How to apply:** Structure reviews with specific numbered findings. Call out mechanical contradictions, degenerate strategies, and narrative/mechanical misalignments directly. Tier findings by severity (blocking / important / design tension). Do not soften findings with excessive hedging or "this could be a great feature if..." framing. Make a clear case for why each issue matters.
