# Dragon Forge Outstanding Game Roadmap

## North Star

Dragon Forge should become a 2D dragon-raising JRPG where overworld exploration, arena battles, dragon progression, and techno-mythic discovery all feed one loop.

The target feel:

`Dragon Warrior overworld + Final Fantasy battle drama + Pokemon-style creature identity + Pern/Close Encounters techno-myth revelation`

The player fantasy:

> I am Skye, a dragon handler discovering that my fantasy world is actually a failing simulation, and my dragons are living system protocols. I explore, train, fight, decode, and slowly become the world's administrator.

## Core Pillars

### 1. A Real Overworld

The map should be the primary play space, not a menu.

Needed:

- Tile-by-tile movement.
- Distinct terrain: forests, mountains, salt flats, ruins, hardware husks, lunar zones.
- Towns, caves, arenas, dungeons, secrets.
- Random or semi-random encounters in dangerous terrain.
- Visible landmarks that make the player curious.
- Unlockable traversal: flight, fog rendering, lunar resonance paths, hardware gates.

### 2. Sticky Battles

Battles need drama, decisions, and animation.

Needed:

- Each dragon type plays differently.
- Attacks have strong animations, anticipation, impact, recovery.
- Enemy intent matters.
- Arena rules matter.
- Status effects: Burn, Corrupt, Overclock, Resonance, Packet Loss, Guard Break.
- Elemental matchups.
- Technique loadouts.
- Bosses with phases.
- Battle rewards that change the map/progression.

### 3. Dragon Identity

Dragons should feel like characters and builds, not skins.

Needed:

- Dragon classes: Magma, Solar, Lunar, Forest, Static, Shadow, and more.
- Stats by species: power, speed, stability, resonance, thermal control.
- Learnable techniques.
- Evolution states.
- Bond level.
- Field abilities.
- Personality and narrative traits.
- Different battle animations and silhouettes.

### 4. Techno-Mythic Story

This is the unique hook. Lean hard into it.

Needed:

- Early game looks fantasy.
- Mid-game reveals simulation and hardware truth.
- B.I.O.S. communicates through light and sound.
- Felix shifts from wizard mentor to panicked sysadmin.
- Dragons are revealed as security and maintenance protocols.
- The Hardware Husk becomes the central mythic artifact.
- Missions blend fantasy and technical absurdity: heat sync, packet fog, root password, MIDI echoes.

### 5. Progression Loop

The loop should be satisfying every five minutes.

Ideal loop:

Explore overworld -> discover arena, dungeon, or quest -> battle or solve mechanic -> earn scraps, items, or techniques -> upgrade dragon/team -> unlock new traversal or battle options -> reach a new region.

## Milestone Plan

### Milestone 1: Playable RPG Spine

Goal: make the game feel like a real RPG in 10 minutes.

Build:

- Proper overworld movement.
- 5-7 visible locations.
- Enter battle from arenas.
- Win, lose, and return flow.
- Dragon technique rewards.
- Basic save/load.
- One dungeon-like area.

Success test:

A player can explore, find a fight, win, get stronger, and want to check the next landmark.

### Milestone 2: Battle Excellence

Goal: battles become the hook.

Build:

- Stronger attack animations.
- Enemy animations.
- Arena rules.
- Status effects.
- Technique loadout limit.
- Dragon switching.
- Three enemy archetypes:
  - Tank/guardian.
  - Fast disruptor.
  - Corrupt/status enemy.
- One real boss with phases.

Success test:

The player should say: "I want to try one more fight."

### Milestone 3: Dragon Progression

Goal: dragons become collectible and buildable.

Build:

- Dragon roster screen.
- Learn and purchase techniques.
- Species traits.
- Bond and level system.
- Evolution or state unlocks.
- Field abilities tied to map traversal.
- Capture or recruit system if the design moves that direction.

Success test:

The player has opinions about which dragon to use.

### Milestone 4: The Hardware Husk Arc

Goal: make the game memorable.

Build:

- Southern Partition region.
- Packet Loss Fog traversal.
- Vault of the First Rack.
- B.I.O.S. light/sound communication.
- Handshake Protocol sequence.
- Scrap-Wraith defense battle.
- World re-render event after success.

Success test:

The player understands the genre shift and feels the "oh wow" moment.

### Milestone 5: Content Expansion

Goal: make it feel like a complete vertical slice.

Build:

- Three overworld regions:
  - Forge Grasslands.
  - Southern Partition.
  - Lunar Sector.
- 8-10 arenas.
- Two dungeons.
- 6-8 dragon types.
- 25-35 techniques.
- Five major missions.
- Three bosses.
- Polished intro/tutorial.

## Next Priorities

Focus in this order:

1. Make overworld movement feel good: tile movement, camera, collision, landmarks, encounter zones.
2. Make battle presentation much better: bigger attack animations, enemy movement, hit effects, readable pacing.
3. Make dragon progression real: technique loadouts, dragon traits, rewards, upgrades.
4. Build one excellent vertical slice: start village -> overworld -> arena -> Felix lab -> Southern Partition -> Hardware Husk -> Handshake -> boss/defense battle.
5. Expand content only after the slice feels good.

## Design Rule

Every system should answer at least one of these:

- Does this make exploration more tempting?
- Does this make battles more exciting?
- Does this make dragons more lovable or useful?
- Does this deepen the techno-mythic reveal?

If not, cut or delay it.

## Recommendation

Build toward a vertical slice, not a pile of isolated features.

Target slice:

Overworld exploration -> one arena -> one dragon upgrade -> one story reveal -> one boss battle -> one map unlock.
