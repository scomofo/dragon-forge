# Act I Finale: The Great Breakout

## Purpose

The Great Breakout is the first-act finale. It combines the Weaver's rescue, the Magma-Core evolution payoff, the first real Mirror Admin retaliation, and the first analog breach of a digital permission wall.

The beat should prove the game's central promise: dragon magic, system logic, and physical hardware all matter, but they solve different problems.

## Sequence

1. **Preparation at the Cooling Intake**
   - The Weaver uses Digital Silk and analog fasteners to craft the Friction Saddle.
   - Felix frames the saddle as practical hardware, not magic: more grip, less drift, better survival in a dive.

2. **Sub-routine Stalker Chase**
   - Mirror Admin dispatches translucent wireframe Ghost Dragons after Skye's Magma-Core compile.
   - They do not use ordinary HP. Their exposed variable is Latency.
   - They can only be hit when their flicker stabilizes for a short window.
   - Contact leeches specialized compile data from the dragon and can eventually revert it toward Root Dragon form.

3. **Southern Partition Gate**
   - The path to the Mainframe is blocked by a red binary curtain stretching from jungle floor to skybox.
   - Direct contact causes a restore-point reset.
   - The gate is a permission check, not a wall, so dragon power cannot burn through it.

4. **Manual Override**
   - Diagnostic Ping reveals a rusted physical access port under simulated stone.
   - The 10mm Wrench pries the bypass switch open.
   - Mirror Admin cannot patch it because the breach is analog and outside the permission layer.

5. **Reveal**
   - The jungle disappears behind Skye.
   - Ahead lies the Tundra of Silicon, a whiteout desert below the Mainframe Spine.

## Design Notes

- This should feel like a breakout, not a dungeon key sequence.
- The 10mm Wrench must feel absurd and powerful: a mundane tool defeating divine-looking red code.
- The Bounty Hunters are not monsters yet. They are the first sign that Mirror Admin has put Skye in the system's threat model.
